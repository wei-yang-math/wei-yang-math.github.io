## Abstract

Let $X$ be a split smooth projective toric scheme over $S = \mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb{Z}$.
We prove that $\mathrm{Perf}(X)$ admits a finite $S$-linear semiorthogonal
decomposition whose components are all equivalent to $\mathrm{Perf}(\mathbb{Z})$,
represented by relative perfect-complex blocks
$M \mapsto \pi^*M \otimes E_i$. As a consequence, for every split base change
$X_R$ one has

$$
K(X_R) \simeq \bigoplus_{i=1}^N K(R)
$$

for nonconnective algebraic $K$-theory. The proof is an integral Cox-VGIT
argument using finite monomial descent and a directed path from an outside
point, lying outside the effective cone and hence having empty semistable
locus, to the target chamber; the auxiliary quotient stacks have finite split
diagonalizable stabilizers, although their acting groups are usually tori.
